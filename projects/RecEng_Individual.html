Business Case Summary

How can we improve customer experience?
What can we do to make their life easier?
What do they actually want when they say that they are looking for easiness and comfort?

These are some of the questions that were, are and will be major painpoints for any company’s strategic planning, whether it is an online platform or bricks and mortar.

The purpose of this Recommendation Engine project, is to propose a solution for one perspective of these questions. Our target is platforms that help people book a table at a restaurant, a bar etc. Based on their previous experiences, which are supposed to be tracked and stored through feedback forms after a complete lunch, dinner etc, we want to give them different beer proposals in order to be able to preorder a specific brand and kind of beer before arriving at the place booked as their next destination.

  • This will help customers personalize and prepare thoroughly their experience, taste something new or make sure that their usual choice is waiting for them.

  • Having a look at the other side also, this will make it easier for businesses to handle their inventory, place their orders according to the demand and also have a better and broadened view of what their customers really like and dislike. Furthermore, this kind of information can contribute to a company’s decision for a change, whether this is the style represented by the place, the actual menu or even the whole market group that they are targeting at.

Market Analysis

The Recommendation Engine we are presenting in this case, comes with the name Book Your Drink. As already mentioned before, the functonality of that will be a personalized drinking experience for customers when booking a table through the client platform. One of our main clients would be Booking.com along with TripAdvisor, which is lately encorporating a reserve a table option for most of the places that are listed in the platform.
Another main factor that makes our software unique is its own existence. There is no similar option around the world web for any popular booking platform.
Regarding competition, while doing the respective research, it is highly notable the fact that this type of recommendation engine is nowhere to be found. There are very few places that provide the customer with an option to preorder a drink. However, even those places host these kind of booking systems in their very own website. Just think for a second though, how many times have you actually entered a bar’s website in order to book your table, not to mention yor drink. I think for most of us Tripadvisor or any other collective platform is the way to go. Adding into the previously mentioned statements, even though a few sites provide the booking drink option, all of them do not offer a recommendation even though they might be collecting data about clients’ preferences.

Data Loading

This report will compare different approaches of a store’s client database, based on several algorithms on the beers dataset. The dataset consists of 50,000 reviews with more than half unique ones. The columns found in this one are:

  • Beer_ID: showing the id of each unique beer brand and kind.
  • Review_Profile_Name: showing the username of the clients that have tasted this specific beer and rated it according their liking or not.
  • Rating: the actual rating that the user placed ranging from 0 to 5, where 0 is the worst and 5 the nest possible rating to be given.

Data Exploration and Processing

At this stage we are checking for missing values in the column review_profilename and we encounter 19 missing fields which we will remove in order not to affect our analysis. Now that our dataset is clean and clear, we rearrage the columns of the matrix in order to convert it to a realRatingMatrix. Below can also be seen 2 summaries:

  • The Average Ratings Per User
  • The Average Ratings Per Beer

Based on these outputs, we are going to take different subsets of our dataframe in order to adjust different types of algorithms for our different kind of customers.

  • Our first split will refer to users with less than 4 ratings (corresponds to about 65% of our customers) and also beers with more than 13 ratings. These users ,based on our business concept are new clients, for which we do not consider to have enough data to try customizing a personalized suggestion. For this case, it is decided to develop a seperate embedded algorithm that will be consisted from POPULAR, UBCF and RANDOM algorithms, wighted in the order that are referred to. Popularity in order to suggest the most demanded beer brands, User Based Collaborative Filtering in order to associate them with other new users and also a random element to help them discover new tastes.

  • Our second will refer to users with more than 10 ratings (corresponds to the next 25% of our customers) and also beers with more than 18 ratings. These users ,based on our business concept are existing and commited clients, for which we do consider to have enough data to try customizing a personalized suggestion. For this case, it is decided to develop another embedded algorithm that will be consisted from IBCF, UBCF and RANDOM algorithms, wighted in the order that are referred to. Item Based Collaborative Filtering in order to correlate users among their beer , User Based Collaborative Filtering in order to associate them with other regular users and also a random element to help them discover new tastes.

  • Our third will refer to users that are loyal (corresponds to the last 10% of our customers) to our platform. Basically, we are referring to those that have more than 25 ratings for beers that have more than 30 ratings. For this section of the market it is decided to use IBCF, UBCF and RANDOM but with a different weight distribution with regard to the regular users, as we have more data on what those customers prefer.Item Based Collaborative Filtering in order to correlate users among their beer, User Based Collaborative Filtering in order to associate them with other new users and also a random element to help them discover new tastes.
